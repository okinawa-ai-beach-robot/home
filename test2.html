Project Outline

Marine debris has a negative impact on biodiversity, e.g. [1], and various studies point to an additional negative economic impact on communities that depend on tourism. We aim for a small-scale collaborative project with the local community, its primary social impact is to demonstrate and spark student interest in research topics and to raise student awareness of environmental issues. The goal of the project is to support local high school students in the organization and execution of independent research in the field of autonomous robots. The students aim to build a prototype robot capable of locomotion, object recognition, and object manipulation in coastal areas. The target application area is twofold: (1) environmental monitoring, and (2) identification and collection of marine debris. The planned work will be carried out and organized by the students under the supervision and guidance of OIST staff, as a result of a collaborative effort between the Doya Unit and the Tani Unit. Further, the project aims at developing and promoting OIST efforts in environmental protection and community involvement.
